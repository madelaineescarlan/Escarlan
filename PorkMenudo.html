Pork Menudo
A hearty traditional Filipino stew that composes of pork meat with potatoes, carrots, and tomatoes.





Pork Menudo Recipe

  • 1/2 kilo Pork shoulder (boneless), cut into cubes
  • 2 small potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 1 can garbanzos beans, chick peas
  • 6 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small onion, peeled and sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled and diced
  • 2 small carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tsp annatto powder
  • 2 small green red bell pepper cored, seeded and sliced
  • 3 1/2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon Canola oil
  • 2 tablespoon fish sauce
  • salt and pepper, to taste

Directions for Cooking

  1. Using a small bowl, dissolve the Annatto powder in 1/2 cup of water, then set aside.
  2. pour Canola oil in a pan over heat.
  3. Add onions and garlic, stirring occasionally, until cooked.
  4. Add pork and fry.
  5. Add fish sauce to the Pork and cook for about 3 minutes.
  6. When the Pork is lightly brown in color, add tomatoes and cook, mash it using the back of spoon, until softened.
  7. Add the Annatto water and remaining water, bring to boil.
  8. Lower the heat, then cover and cook for about 40 minutes or until pork is tender.
  9. Add the garbanzos, potatoes, and carrots, then mix well.
  10. Cover and continue to simmer for around 5 minutes or until potatoes and carrots are tender.
  11. Add the green bell peppers, raisins and liver then cook for another 4 minutes or until liver is cooked.
  12. Season with salt and pepper in preference to your taste.
  13. Remove from heat, Serve and Enjoy!
