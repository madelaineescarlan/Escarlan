Kare-Kare
A popular Filipino stew that consists of simmered oxtail, vegetables and thick savory peanut-based sauce.





Kare-Kare Recipe

  • 1 kg oxtail, cut into serving sizes
  • 1 pc banana heart (puso ng saging), cut and pre-soaked for 20 minutes
  • 1 bundle string beans (sitaw), cut into 3 inch sizes
  • 1 bundle pechay or bok choy
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1 pc medium-sized onion
  • 1/4 cup rice flour or ground rice
  • 1 1/2 tbsp annatto powder
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • bagoong alamang (shrimp paste), served at the side

Directions for Cooking

  1. Rinse oxtail under running water and drain well.
  2. Place in a deep cooking pot and put enough water to cover it.
  3. Boil water in medium heat and remove the scum that rises.
  4. Lower the heat and add more water, making sure to maintain about 6 cups of water as it will be used for the broth later.
  5. Cover and simmer for about 2 hours or until the oxtails is tender or can be pierced by a fork.
  6. Drain the oxtail and reserve 6 cups of the broth then set aside.
  7. For the vegetables, bring about 4 cups of water to boil in a cooking pot.
  8. Blanch the banana heart for 1 minute then remove and set aside.
  9. Blanch the eggplant for 1 minute then remove and set aside.
  10. Blanch the string beans for 1 minute then remove and set aside.
  11. Blanch the pechay/ bok choy for 30 seconds then remove and set aside.
  12. Place the rice flour in a skillet or frying pan and apply medium heat.
  13. Cook the rice flour, while stirring occasionally, until lightly browned and toasted.
  14. Combine the toasted rice flour and 1/4 cup of the reserved broth in a bowl then whisk until smooth.
  15. In a small bowl, combine the annatto powder with another 1/4 cup of the reserved broth then stir until dissolved.
  16. In another bowl, combine the peanut butter with 1/2 cup of the reserved broth then stir until well blended.
  17. Pour the cooking oil in a large cooking pot in medium heat. When the oil is hot enough, add the onion and garlic then cook until soft and aromatic.
  18. Add the oxtail. Cook and stir occasionally until lightly browned.
  19. Add the fish sauce then cook for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  20. Add the remaining 5 cups of the reserved broth and bring to a simmer.
  21. Add the rice flour mixture, the annatto powder mixture, and the peanut butter mixture and stir well to combine.
  22. Season with salt and pepper, preference to your taste.
  23. Continue to cook for 10 minutes or until the sauce slightly thickens.
  24. Add the blanched vegetables and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes.
  25. Remove from heat and serve with shrimp paste on the side, Enjoy!
